Lalith Munasinghe

Founder and Principal

Lalith is the Founder and Principal of Talenteck LLC. He is a Professor of Economics at Barnard College and the School of International and Public Affairs at Columbia University, where he specializes in Labor Economics, Microeconomic Theory, and Applied Econometrics. Lalith received his Ph.D. from Columbia University in Economics in 1995, and holds two B.A.s: one from Princeton University in Philosophy, and a second from Cambridge University, England, in Economics. Lalith has been working on human capital analytics for the last 20 years, beginning with his dissertation “Studies in the Dynamics of Labor Turnover.” His academic career has focused on the theoretical and practical foundations of human capital and matching theories, and his publications include articles in the Journal of Labor Economics, Journal of Political Economy, and Labour Economics, among others. At Barnard, Lalith produced a consulting analysis of the gender gap in faculty tenure rates using several decades of panel data. He also analyzed the drivers and outcomes of college applicants: who applies, who is admitted, who accepts, and who becomes top academic performers.


Lalith’s long consulting career in human capital strategy includes projects with Mitchell Madison Inc., William M. Mercer Inc., IntelliRisk Management Corp, and the World Bank. He was the analytics leader at iQor Inc., a financial services company, for four years. He has written several white papers on human capital products, transforming his academic interests into viable commercial frameworks. At iQor, Lalith constructed and implemented a suite of human capital products related to turnover, performance, compensation, recruiting, and staffing. Also at iQor, Lalith developed and implemented consumer matching algorithms, consumer contact and liquidity models, and call-center dialer algorithms.
